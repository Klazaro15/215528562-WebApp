Ube Jam

The Ube Jam of Baguio City, particularly from the Good Shepherd Convent, is a reowned delicacy made from purple yam. The history of Good Shepherd's ube Jam dates back to 1976 when Sister Fidelis Atienza began experimenting with the recie, leading to the creation of a creamy and flavourful jam.

Quality Assurance

To maintain the quality of the ube jam, a key aspect is the continuousinnovation in maintaining the availability of quality planting materials year-round. This innovation ensures a consistent supply of high-quality ingredients for the jam, eliminating the need to wait for specific harvest period. The prodution process at the convent involves using quality purple yam without food coloring, preserving the authentic flavor and quality of the ube jam.
